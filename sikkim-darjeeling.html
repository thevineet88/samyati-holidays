Package Overview

The Himalayas at Their Most Spectacular

This 7-day tour packs in more than most people see in a lifetime. Gangtok's bustling MG Marg, the silence of Tsomgo Lake at 12,400 ft, standing at Nathu La Pass on the India-China border at 17,000 ft, the toy train that chugs through Darjeeling's tea gardens, and that famous Tiger Hill sunrise with Kanchenjunga glowing pink in the background. We handle everything including flights so you just show up at Mumbai airport.

Altitude Advisory

Nathu La Pass is at 17,000 ft. Our itinerary is designed for proper acclimatisation. We advise consulting your doctor if you have heart or respiratory conditions. Our guide carries basic altitude medicine.

Day by Day

Detailed Itinerary

Morning flight from Mumbai to Bagdogra (via Kolkata). Arrive Bagdogra by afternoon. Meet our local representative. Scenic 4–5 hour drive to Gangtok through the Teesta river valley, your first taste of the Northeast's stunning landscapes. Check into hotel, welcome dinner.
Full day exploring Gangtok: MG Marg pedestrian promenade (the heart of Gangtok), Enchey Monastery with views of the Himalayas, Flower Exhibition Centre, the Gangtok Ropeway for aerial views, Do-Drul Chorten (iconic stupa). Evening strolling on MG Marg, great street food and local handicrafts.
Drive to Tsomgo Lake (12,400 ft), a glacial lake that changes colour with the seasons, sacred to the Sikkimese people. Continue to Baba Mandir (13,200 ft), a shrine dedicated to a soldier whose spirit is said to guard the border. The drive itself through high-altitude rhododendron forests is breathtaking. Return to Gangtok by evening.
The most unforgettable day of the tour. Early morning drive to Nathu La Pass, the ancient Silk Route pass at 17,000 ft on the India-China border. Stand at the actual border, look into Tibet. Indian Army jawans on one side, Chinese PLA on the other. The raw wind and silence at this altitude is indescribable. Permits arranged by Samyati in advance. Return to Gangtok for the night.
Drive from Gangtok to Darjeeling (~3 hours). En route, visit Rumtek Monastery, the largest monastery in Sikkim, a seat of Tibetan Buddhism. Check into Darjeeling hotel. Evening stroll on Mall Road with views of the Himalayan range. Try Darjeeling's famous first-flush tea at a local café.
4 AM wake-up call for Tiger Hill, non-negotiable. Drive 11 km to watch the sunrise paint Kanchenjunga (8,586 m) in shades of gold and pink. Words cannot do it justice. Return via Batasia Loop (a famous spiral railway loop with a war memorial). Late morning: ride the UNESCO-listed Darjeeling Himalayan Railway toy train, one of the most charming train journeys in the world. Afternoon: visit a tea garden, see the tea-making process. Farewell dinner.
Morning at leisure, last breakfast in Darjeeling, some souvenir shopping (Darjeeling tea, Tibetan handicrafts). Drive to Bagdogra airport. Evening flight to Mumbai. Arrive home with memories that will last forever.
